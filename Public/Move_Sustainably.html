How to Move Sustainably (Even Without Us)

Tulip ReMoving was founded with the aim of making home moves more sustainable for everyone. Even if you are unable to book with us, there are many steps you can take to reduce the carbon footprint of your move.

In the UK, the government has committed to reaching net zero carbon emissions by 2050. House moves may seem small in comparison, but the vans, packaging and waste generated during a move all contribute to environmental impact. By making conscious choices, you can help reduce emissions and waste while saving money and protecting your community’s environment.

This guide covers three key areas:

  • Packing sustainably
  • Transporting your belongings responsibly
  • Disposing of unwanted items in an environmentally friendly way

We hope you find these tips helpful.

Packing Sustainably

Sustainable packing not only helps protect the environment but can also reduce your costs (which often reach £100–£200 for packaging) and keep your belongings safe in all weather conditions. Consider the following approaches:

  • Use what you already own: Suitcases, rucksacks, laundry baskets and even instrument cases can be excellent alternatives to cardboard boxes. If you don’t have enough, consider borrowing from friends or family.
  • Choose reusable materials: Some moving companies now rent durable, reusable crates and protective covers made from recycled plastic. At Tulip ReMoving, we exclusively use reusable packing solutions.
  • Opt for recycled cardboard: Cardboard is widely recyclable, and its recycling process uses 75% less energy than producing it from virgin wood. Read more about cardboard recycling here. Always check local rules, as recycling guidelines differ between boroughs.

Transporting Your Belongings Sustainably

A typical 20-mile move using a diesel van consumes several litres of fuel and produces a significant amount of CO2. While electric trucks are not yet widespread in the removals industry, more companies (including Tulip ReMoving) now use fully electric vans. In the UK, switching to electric transport can cut the carbon footprint of a move by up to 80%, even after accounting for the electricity grid mix. Here’s an in-depth article on the UK electricity mix and emissions.

Discarding Unwanted Items Sustainably

  • Repair: If items are broken, consider repairing them rather than throwing them away. Repair Cafés now operate across the UK and provide free help to bring items back to life.
  • Donate or regift: Many charities and community organisations welcome donations of furniture, clothing and household items. For example, British Heart Foundation and Sue Ryder can even collect large items free of charge.
  • Recycle responsibly: Recycling rules vary by borough. Always check your local council’s guidance before disposing of items. The UK Parliament has also published a useful overview of household waste collection in England and Wales.

At Tulip ReMoving, we ensure as many unwanted items as possible are resold, donated, or recycled, helping you move with peace of mind while reducing waste.
